Waffle

Waffles are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, made from a rich batter of flour, eggs, milk, and butter. Cooked in a waffle iron, they form a unique grid pattern that perfectly holds syrup, fruits, chocolate, or whipped cream. Waffles are a classic breakfast and brunch favorite, loved for their delightful texture and ability to be enjoyed sweet or savory with endless topping possibilities.

Ingredient

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 3/4 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup melted butter or oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, beat the eggs, then add the milk, melted butter (or oil), and vanilla extract.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined (batter may be slightly lumpy).
  4. Preheat the waffle maker and lightly grease it with butter or oil.
  5. Pour enough batter into the waffle maker to cover the surface evenly.
  6. Close the lid and cook until the waffles are golden brown and crisp.
  7. Carefully remove the waffles and serve hot with syrup, fruit, or toppings of your choice.
